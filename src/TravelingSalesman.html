Travelling Salesman Problem — GA, Simulated Annealing & ACO

Python Ant Colony Optimization Genetic Algorithm Simulated Annealing NP-Hard Optimization Heuristic Search Pheromone Modeling Hyperparameter Tuning TSPLIB Datasets


A group AI project comparing three optimization algorithms against the classic Travelling Salesman Problem — finding the shortest route visiting every city exactly once. Four TSPLIB datasets of increasing difficulty were used: 5, 26, 42, and 48 cities, representing a toy problem, France, the Dantzig 42 reduction, and the contiguous US state capitals. Each algorithm was tested on all four maps and results were compared against known optimal scores. My contribution focused on the Ant Colony Optimization algorithm and the project report.

The three approaches — Genetic Algorithm, Simulated Annealing, and Ant Colony Optimization — each represent fundamentally different optimization strategies. All three solved the 5 and 26 city problems optimally. At scale (42 and 48 cities), SA proved the most consistently accurate, never exceeding 1.43% above optimal. ACO and GA both found near-optimal solutions for 42 cities (0.71% off) but diverged more on 48 cities as problem complexity grew.

Algorithm Breakdown

Genetic Algorithm — Crossover & Mutation

A population of candidate routes evolves over generations. Each route's fitness score is its total distance — normalized so shorter paths score higher. Crossover splices two parent routes by a random index range; mutation randomly swaps segments between children. A selectivity variable filters weak parents before reproduction, which proved critical for stabilising fitness at scale. Optimal settings for larger maps: population 70, mutation rate 0.3, selectivity 15%. Found solutions within 10% of optimal almost immediately, but struggled to close that final gap on 42+ cities.

Simulated Annealing — Controlled Exploration

Modelled on metallurgical annealing — a "temperature" parameter controls willingness to accept worse solutions, allowing escape from local optima. Temperature decays as (total_iter − iter) / temp_mod. With temp_mod=3000, SA matched optimal scores for both 5 and 26 cities and came within 1.43% and 1.08% for 42 and 48 cities respectively — the strongest scaling performance of the three algorithms. Runtime scaled reasonably: 6s → 27s → 45s → 76s across the four datasets.

Ant Colony Optimization — Pheromone Pathfinding

Ants spawn in different cities and choose their next city based on both distance and pheromone trail strength. Short paths accumulate more pheromone, biasing future ants toward them. Key tunable variables: colony size (96), iterations (200), initial pheromone weight, and evaporation rate. ACO matched optimal on 5 and 26 cities and was only 0.71% off on 42 cities — but diverged to 3.59% on 48 cities. The main implementation challenge was tracking individual ant distances and ensuring pheromone evaporation was calibrated correctly across all path lengths.
