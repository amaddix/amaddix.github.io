About Me

I didn't walk into my computer science degree with complete certainty — I walked in curious, and a little nervous. What I found on the other side completely changed me.

From the moment I started building, I knew this was it. There's nothing quite like starting from a blank file and crafting something that actually works — designing the structure, breaking everything down to its smallest moving parts, finding the cleanest way to organize it all. It feels like solving a puzzle. A puzzle that, without fail, breaks on you.

Sure, a stubborn bug at midnight can make me want to lose my mind. But tracking it down — figuring out exactly what went wrong and why — is genuinely one of the most satisfying feelings I know.

I'm a fullstack developer with a natural pull toward the backend. I love architecting the core of a system — the logic, the data flow, the foundation everything else depends on. But I care deeply about the front end too, because a backend is only as good as the cohesive, polished experience it delivers. Clean design isn't an afterthought to me — it's part of the craft.

Outside of software, I travel as much as I can — drawn to places with striking geology and rich history. Angels Landing, Grinnell Glacier, Antelope Canyon. I don't shy away from the brutal hikes. And yes, I'm absolutely the person stopping mid-trail to look at every bird and bug — I find wonder in the details everywhere I look, and that curiosity never really turns off.
